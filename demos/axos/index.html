📷 Image strategy — where to place photography for production
Demo uses SVG patterns Replace with real photos for production
Hero background (highest priority)
  • Recommended image: Aspirational lifestyle — person confidently checking their phone/laptop showing a savings balance. Or an aerial view of a mountain summit (ties directly to "Summit" brand name). Clean, optimistic, financially aspirational tone.
  • Technical spec: 1920×1080px minimum, JPG, ~200–400KB. The gradient overlay (already in place) will reduce it to 55–92% opacity so the text stays readable on any background.
  • Overlay already coded: A multi-stop gradient overlay is built into the hero now — it gets darker toward the left where the headline lives, and lighter toward the right where the calculator is. This keeps both sides readable regardless of photo content.
  • Implementation: Add background-image: url('your-photo.jpg') to the .hero-bg CSS class. The overlay layer above it handles contrast automatically.
Steps section background (medium priority)
  • Recommended image: Axos mobile app screenshot or lifestyle shot of someone on their phone. Placed on the right half of the section as a subtle reinforcement of "open in 3 minutes from your phone."
  • Technical spec: 1400×800px, low-saturation works best against the dark navy. Keep image to right 40% of frame to avoid overlapping centered text.
  • Overlay: A radial gradient overlay is already in place to fade the image toward the text area — no additional work needed.
InsureGuard+ section (lower priority, high value)
  • Recommended image: Abstract representation of security/protection — a vault door, a shield, or an architectural/institutional photo. This section targets high-balance customers so the visual tone should feel premium and serious.
  • Technical spec: 1200×800px, dark or desaturated image works best. Right-side placement, with diamond/hexagon geometric overlay already in place as placeholder.
Sections that do NOT need photos
  • Trust bar: Clean navy background. Icons/stats should stand alone without photo distraction.
  • No-catch grid: White background with teal icon cards. Photo would compete with the card content.
  • Comparison table: Data-heavy — photo backgrounds reduce readability of table cells.
  • FAQ: White background. Clarity over decoration.
  • Final CTA: The large rate number (3.75%) is the visual hero here. A photo background would dilute its impact. Dark navy + amber is the right call.
Stock photo sources to recommend
  • Unsplash: free, high quality, commercial license — search "savings", "financial freedom", "mountain summit", "mobile banking"
  • Getty / Shutterstock: for a licensed production asset. Budget ~$50–200/image.
  • Axos brand library: check if Axos has existing approved lifestyle photography from their marketing team before sourcing externally.
