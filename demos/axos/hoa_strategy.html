01 · Landing Page Strategy

Match the page to the source, not the product.

Axos already maintains separate landing pages for Google, LinkedIn, and Meta traffic — the infrastructure is in place. The opportunity is in the content: today, all three pages share the same H1 ("Smarter banking for your community is finally here") and the same "Submit" CTA. A Google searcher looking for "HOA loans" is in a completely different state than a property manager scrolling LinkedIn — each page should speak to how that visitor arrived.

Google · High Intent

Direct, keyword-rich, authoritative

User is actively searching for a solution. Match the query, show the product, shorten the path to contact.

Example H1 Comprehensive HOA Banking, Lending & Treasury Management Solutions Tailored lending and automated treasury solutions designed for the unique financial needs of community associations.
LinkedIn · Professional B2B

Scalability, efficiency, partnership

User is in professional research mode — benchmarking vendors, evaluating partners, thinking in terms of their portfolio.

Example H1 Scale Your Property Management Portfolio with Integrated Banking Services Streamline operations and drive efficiency with a banking partner that understands your portfolio's growth requirements.
Meta · Interruptive

Pattern interrupt on a daily frustration

User is scrolling passively. Lead with a concrete pain point they feel every week, not a brand statement.

Example H1 Stop Manual Reconciliation — Automate Your Community's Receivables Integrate your management software with Axos Bank to eliminate manual data entry and free up staff for higher-value work.
02 · Prospecting Engine

ZoomInfo into social, then sales.

Build a synchronized pipeline so that by the time a Relationship Manager reaches out, the prospect has already seen Axos three to five times in their LinkedIn and Meta feeds. Outbound stops being cold.

01

Identify

ZoomInfo firmographic filtering on HOA management companies by units managed, region, and C-suite/board titles, combined with intent monitoring on "HOA banking" and "ECR yield" research spikes. Output: a daily-synced pipeline surfacing prospects at peak research.

02

Ingest

Direct API sync from ZoomInfo MarketingOS into LinkedIn Matched Audiences and Meta Custom Audiences. Export both work and personal identifiers to close the business vs. personal email gap and lift match rates meaningfully above the Meta industry average.

03

Warm

Non-interruptive air cover on Axos differentiators — Vantaca and CINC integrations, ECR benefits. Sales outreach is delayed until the prospect has spent 7–10 days in the warming pool. The "Who is Axos?" hurdle is already gone when the RM calls.

Salesforce routing

Leads from Meta and LinkedIn flow instantly into Salesforce, where assignment rules route them to the correct Regional Relationship Manager by territory. For audiences that fall below platform minimums, expand from single intent-trigger contacts to the full ZoomInfo buying committee to meet threshold.

03 · Inbound Search & Tealium Activation

Capture intent. Qualify with behavior. Convert with precision.

Google Search captures active demand; Tealium identifies who is actually qualified based on how they engage with the site; RLSA and Meta retargeting concentrate spend on the visitors most likely to close.

01

Capture

Paid search dominates top-of-page results for high-value HOA banking queries — treasury management, lockbox, lines of credit, assessment and capital improvement loans.

02

Qualify

Tealium badges visitors based on behavior — three visits to the Lending page, a guide download, a pricing scroll. Anonymous traffic becomes structured audience data.

03

Convert

Badges fuel RLSA bid boosts on Google and specific product-CTA retargeting on Meta. Spend concentrates on known, qualified prospects instead of repeat cold traffic.

Three-campaign architecture

Campaign 1

Active prospecting

Audience: Cold traffic only. Exclude Tealium-badged visitors, ZoomInfo lists, and existing customers.

Themes: High-intent pain-point keywords — HOA lockbox, assessment loans, treasury management.

Message: "The specialized bank for community associations" — expertise and HOA-specific features.

Campaign 2 · Priority

High-intent retargeting

Audience: Tealium-badged visitors, stale lead hashed lists, ZoomInfo intent lists.

Strategy: Aggressive bidding when known prospects re-enter the search market. Defend on competitor brand searches.

Message: "Finish your application" or "See why 35,000+ associations trust Axos" — trust, social proof, ease of switching.

Campaign 3

Competitor conquesting

Audience: Cold traffic searching for direct competitors.

Strategy: Appear above competitor organic listings on branded searches.

Message: "Switching made simple" — lead with Vantaca and CINC integrations and the dedicated Relationship Manager model.

Closed-loop lead flow

Meta Lead Ads capture low-friction intent; Salesforce routes and enriches; Tealium suppresses converted leads from future spend automatically.

Lead-capture enhancement

The only current CTA is "Schedule a Call" — high effort for early-stage researchers. Add a mid-funnel offer such as "Download the 2026 HOA Financial Automation Guide." Anonymous visitors become identified contacts in Tealium, which unlocks the full retargeting and nurture sequence above.

04 · SEO & AI / LLM Readiness

Be the source AI models cite.

Traditional SEO rewards the site that answers the question. AI search rewards the site that is structured, verified, and most talked about. Both point to the same technical and content work — done once, it compounds across Google, ChatGPT, Perplexity, and AI Overviews.

Technical architecture

  • Structured data. Deploy FinancialService and FAQPage schema so LLMs can identify Axos rates, integrations, and service areas without guessing from page copy.
  • AI-extractable formatting. Convert ECR tiers and loan requirements into clean HTML tables and bulleted lists — the formats AI models preferentially extract for answers.
  • Entity association. Earn citations on high-authority industry sites with links back to pillar content. AI recommends what is most frequently referenced.
  • Named authorship. Attach Relationship Manager bylines and LinkedIn profiles to thought leadership. "Expert-verified" content is ranked higher than anonymous corporate copy.

A visible gap to close first

The current "Integrated Software Partners" section relies on logos alone. AI crawlers cannot read image files to confirm compatibility, so Axos is effectively invisible on queries like "Which banks integrate with Vantaca?"

Add a text description for each partner: "Axos integrates with Vantaca to provide real-time, API-driven account reconciliation." Same logos, same design — but now the claim is machine-readable.

Pair this with a natural-language FAQ section targeting queries like "How does an ECR offset HOA banking fees?" and "What are the benefits of a specialized HOA lockbox?" to capture "People Also Ask" placements and feed direct answers to LLM synthesis.

05 · First-Party Data Activation

Use the data Axos already owns.

Two first-party assets are underutilized today: stale prospect lists from prior campaigns and the active, high-LTV HOA client base. Both can be activated across Meta and Google through hashed email matching.

Existing Asset

Stale prospect re-engagement

Upload hashed email lists of past prospects to Google and Meta. Messaging leads with "Welcome back" or "What's new" — e.g., "Our Vantaca integration is now live."

Meta Lead Ads with pre-filled fields make re-capture low friction. On Google, bid aggressively when stale prospects search for competitors. This converts spend already incurred into net-new pipeline.

Net-New Prospecting

Lookalike & similar audiences

Seed with hashed email lists of the highest-LTV active HOA clients. Model 1%–2% Lookalike on Meta and Similar Audiences on Google.

Lead with educational, thought-leadership creative to build trust before the prospect enters an active search phase. Budget concentrates on audiences with the highest statistical probability of conversion.

06 · Meta Conversion Optimization

A safety net for mobile bounce.

High-intent prospects drop off on mobile when forms demand manual entry. Meta Lead Ads act as a secondary capture path for the qualified visitor who doesn't complete "Submit" on the site form.

Why it works

  • Pre-filled fields (name, business email, phone) reduce errors and improve CRM data quality from day one.
  • Native Meta → Salesforce API connector delivers leads in real time — no CSV uploads, no delay between capture and RM follow-up.
  • Automated Salesforce routing assigns each lead to the correct Regional Relationship Manager by territory or firmographics.

Where it fits in the funnel

Lead Ads are not a replacement for the source-aligned landing pages. They are the fallback layer that captures the high-intent mobile user who clicked through, started engaging, and stalled at the form. Paired with Tealium suppression, they add volume without cannibalizing existing conversions.

07 · Measurement Framework

What to watch, and what to avoid publishing too early.

Each workstream has a primary metric and a directional goal. Specific CPA and conversion-rate targets should be held until a clean baseline exists — publishing a target before baseline sets the program up to be judged against a guess.

Primary · Inbound

CPA: badged vs. cold

Compare cost-per-acquisition for Tealium-badged audiences against unqualified cold traffic. Badged audiences should show meaningfully lower CPA. Establish baseline before committing to a target number.

Primary · Landing Pages

Conversion & bounce by source

Track conversion rate and bounce rate separately for Google, LinkedIn, and Meta. Source-aligned pages should outperform the uniform experience on both metrics for their respective channels.

Prospecting Engine

Sales responsiveness

Measurable lift in sales response rates and matched-audience engagement after prospects pass through the 7–10 day LinkedIn and Meta warming sequence.

Data Activation

Stale & lookalike recovery

Re-engagement rate on stale lists and conversion rate of lookalike audiences vs. generic cold prospecting — both should outperform unmatched audiences.
