Identify Your Custodial Friction Points

Is your custodian driving business growth or acting as an operational drag? Preview the five major red flags below.

1

Lack of Personalized Support

When you feel like "just another number" to your custodian, your firm's growth is deprioritized. Many massive legacy platforms prioritize their largest institutional accounts, leaving mid-size independent RIAs with automated response queues and minimal live support.

10% - 20%
of RIAs at the legacy "Big Three" are actively exploring alternative relationships (T3 Survey).
What you can do:
  • Document service gap times (e.g. response delays, processing bottlenecks).
  • Push for a dedicated, white-glove support team or relationship manager.

Get the full PDF to audit your custodian.

2

Bureaucratic, Unresponsive Processes

Slow response times for basic account openings, wire approvals, or technical queries create significant operational drag. When internal custodial processes require navigating multiple siloed departments, your client experience suffers.

92%
of advisors would switch custodians due to poor technology setups (Advisor360 Survey).
What you can do:
  • Track processing metrics for new account setups and money transfers.
  • Explore custodians built on open API architecture for seamless CRM integrations.
Download Action Checklist

Get the full PDF to audit your custodian.

3

Competing Retail Priorities

One of the biggest concerns for growing RIAs is strategic misalignment. Many large custodians operate direct-to-consumer (DTC) retail arms that compete directly with the advisors they claim to support. This conflict of interest means your client base is constantly marketed to by your own platform provider.

DTC Conflict
Your custodian should be a partner, not a direct competitor for client assets.
What you can do:
  • Check if your custodian cross-sells services directly to your clients.
  • Consider diversifying with a strictly business-to-business (B2B) custodian.

Get the full PDF to audit your custodian.

4

Rigid, Standardized Offerings

Growing advisory firms require custom options. Standardized, one-size-fits-all offerings limit your ability to serve high-net-worth clients who require complex lending, integrated cash management, or banking-related services alongside traditional investments.

60% +
of firms serve both high-net-worth and mass-affluent segments, needing modular tools (IAA Snapshot).
What you can do:
  • Review your custodian's flexibility in integrating third-party banking and credit tools.
  • Choose providers offering customizable services that adapt as your client demographics shift.

Get the full PDF to audit your custodian.

5

Fragmented Relationship Management

When contact structures rotate constantly, communication breaks down. Being forced to repeat basic business details or issues multiple times to dynamic call center reps is a major drain on operational efficiency.

Institutional Drag
Rotating support structures disrupt relationship trust and delay critical problem solving.
What you can do:
  • Audit current communication pathways: track if you have a single point of escalation.
  • Evaluate alternative platforms built around dedicated, team-based relationship structures.
